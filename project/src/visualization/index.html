    Description

    In this project, a web-based network flow graph is constructed to visualize IP connection data collected at the Universidad Del Cauca across six days in April and May of 2017 [1]. An interactive graph of ongoing connections at user-specified points in time are rendered showing the rate and composition of data flows between hosts in the network.

    The number of flows is selectable in the red drop-down box in the top left, performance may degrade with a large number of nodes. The timestamp of interest is selectable from the blue timeline bar, it shows a random sampling of network flows active at that particular time in the graph.

    The graph may be resized, panned and zoomed to explore different sections of the network. The direction of change on an edge represents direction of initiation of the data flow. The width of edges linearly represents the magnitude of the average data flow relative to the minimum and maximum of all paths. The legend colour keys are used to distinguish the application layer protocol the flow was identified as via deep packet inspection.
